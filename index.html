The short version

I spent two years at Google shipping features to a billion users. Then I went to the UN to build a climate data portal. Then I joined RMSI and shipped two government apps from scratch for the state of Assam. Somewhere along the way, I stopped caring about the code and started caring about the problem. Now I'm in the space between what I was and what I'm becoming — exploring PropTech, AI, and what it means to build something from scratch.

What's next

Every role I've had has pulled me closer to the problem and further from the terminal. At Google I learned scale. At the UN I learned stakeholder alignment. At RMSI I learned what 0-to-1 actually feels like when the users are forest rangers in Assam with intermittent connectivity. I don't want to go back to just building features. I want to own the whole thing — not just the what and why, but the business underneath it.
